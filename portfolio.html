Final Year project - AI Volume Controller

    Controlling volume using hand gestures typically involves using a device equipped with a camera or other sensors to detect and interpret hand movements. The process generally follows these steps:

    Hand Gesture Detection: The device captures a live feed or images of the user's hand through a camera. Computer vision algorithms analyze these images to detect specific hand gestures.

    Gesture Recognition: The detected hand gestures are recognized and mapped to predefined commands. For volume control, common gestures might include swiping up or down to increase or decrease the volume, respectively.

    Communication with Audio System: The recognized gestures trigger commands that are communicated to the audio system, such as a computer, smart TV, or speaker. This communication is often done through wireless protocols like Bluetooth or through a direct connection.

    Adjustment of Volume Levels: The audio system receives the commands and adjusts the volume levels accordingly. For example, if a user swipes their hand upward, the volume increases, and if they swipe downward, the volume decreases.

    Feedback Mechanism: To enhance user experience, there may be feedback mechanisms, such as visual or auditory cues, indicating that the volume has been successfully adjusted based on the detected hand gesture.
